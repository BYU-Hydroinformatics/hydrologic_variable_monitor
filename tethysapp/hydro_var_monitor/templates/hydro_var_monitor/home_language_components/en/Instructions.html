Instructions

Welcome to the Climate Trends App!

The Climate Trends App allows you to visualize satellite information for the last 30 years and compare them for variables of interest.

To start, select a variable of interest: precipitation, temperature, soil moisture, or evapotranspiration. Then choose the satellite you want to use for your chosen variable.

By clicking “Load Map” you can visualize a map for that variable and satellite.

Next, select an area of interest, a province in Ecuador or latitude and longitude coordinates.

Select a point or area of interest:

1. Coordinates: Click the button “Enter Lat/Lon”, and a screen will pop up where you can enter the latitude and longitude coordinates for a point. Click “Save”, and then “Show Graph” to see a graph of the results at that point.

2. Region of Interest: In the upper left-hand corner of map there is an option with two buttons to draw on the map. Select one of the two buttons to draw your area of interest on the map. Click the button “Show Graph” to visualize the results.

3. Ecuador Province: At the button of the sidebar menu, choose the button ¨Load Region Data¨ and select the province of interest from the drop-down meny. Finally, click the button “Load Region Data” to visualize the results.

Visualize Results

Once you have selected a variable, satellite and area of interest you can visualize the results on a graph. You can download the results as a .csv, by clicking the button “Download”. You can also download the graph in .png format by clicking the camera button located in the top right corner of the graph.

On the graph, you can select a date by clicking and selecting a range of dates. You can visualize the original graph again by clicking on Autoscale, which is located near the camera button.

You can turn on and off the lines of the graph by clicking on the legend. By default all lines are on.

Compare averages for a variable

Once you have selected a variable, satellite and area of interest you can compare the averages of the variable for different satellites. To do this, click on the button “Compare Averages” located at the bottom of the sidebar menu.

Select a Specific Date

You can visualize the results by clicking the button “Use a Specific Year”.
